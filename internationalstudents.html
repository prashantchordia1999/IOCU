English Language Requirements


At CHRIST (Deemed to be University) all the courses are taught in English medium, it is essential that the students are proficient in the English language.

International students coming from non English speaking background must :
a) Provide evidence of passing the qualifying examination in English.
b) Have passed English tests like TOEFL(paper) , TOEFL (online) or IELTS with scores of 550 , 213 and 6.0 respectively.

If not , the students must undergo the 3 month Intensive English language Course (IELC) conducted by the University from March to May 2018. This course is a full time course conducted from Monday to Friday from 9:00am to 4:00pm for a total duration of more than 320 hours.
It is designed to help the foreign students to cope with the academic standards in CHRIST ( Deemed to be University)
This course includes basic structures of English language like

a) Functional Grammar (80 hours)
b) Reading skills (80 hours)
c) listening and speaking skills (80 hours)
d) writing skills (80 hours)

Fees for the course including study material will be US $ 400/- ( non-refundable)

Accommodation

    The University offers a variety of accommodation options for students, located around and close to the campus to suit different needs and budgets. Students are free to choose the lodging which suits them best. Limited seats are available for the students opting for the housing/hostel facilities. The cost for the hostel room varies from US $600 - $1000 per year depending on the type of room .
    The international Office strives to render help in helping the students find accommodation. It is preferred that the students live in proximity to the campus so as to take full advantage of the facilities the University has to offer. The cost of living is estimated to be upto US $3000 - $4000 per year depending on the type of accommodation and the external expenses the student opts for.

Special Regulations

    All students of Foreign National (except from Nepal and Bhutan) admitted to the University are required to arrive in India with a valid Passport and Student Visa specifying “For at Christ University, Bengaluru”
    2. No student of Foreign National (except from Nepal and Bhutan) will be permitted to attend classes at the University without Valid Passport, Student Visa and Residential Permit.
    3. Students of Foreign National who are already in India studying in other educational institutions have to obtain ‘No Objection Certificate’ (NOC) from their present institutions and enclose the same with their application form. After obtaining Provisional Admission Letter from the University, they have to change the Student Visa in favour of CHRIST ( Deemed to be University) at the local Foreigners Regional Registration Office, Bengaluru via online portal www.e-FRRO before they start attending classes at CHRIST (Deemed to be University)
    4. Within 14 days of arrival in India, Foreign students are required to register with the local Foreigners Regional Registration Office (FRRO) on the online portal www.e-FRRO concerned via viz.

    Foreigners Regional Registration Officer,
    Bureau of Immigration,
    (MHA) Government of India,
    TTMC A Block, 5th floor, BMTC Bus Stand Building ,
    KH Road , Shanti Nagar, Bengaluru. - 560027
    www.immigrationindia.nic.in

    5. The following documents are required to be enclosed in the prescribed application for registration.
    a) Original valid passport and visa copy
    b) Bonafide certificate from the University
    c) C Form from the PG owner/ Landlord/ Warden however for students living inside the University campus , the C form will be issued by the International office .
    d) Three passport size photograph with white background
    e) Proof of residence of the applicant (rental agreement) and ID card of the landlord ( Driving license or Election ID Card or Ration card copy)
    f) Bank statement / ATM card copy and mini statement.
    g) Course fee Receipt.
    6. A copy of the Residential Permit obtained from the FRRO has to be deposited by Foreign Students at the International Office of the University , without fail.
    7. Application for renewal of student visa if applicable and Residential Permit has to be made well in advance and any changes in the Residential Address have to be brought to the notice of the FRRO.


Campus Life at CHRIST (Deemed to be University)


    ● State of the Art Infrastructure amidst greenery is the Hallmark of the University. With a wide variety of plants and trees , a Bird’s Park , a greenhouse , a choice of seminar halls , a 2000 seater auditorium, well equipped laboratories, secure hostels and modern gymnasiums for men and women, Wi-Fi enabled campus, audio-visual enabled classrooms and multi-sports grounds add to the ambience of the University.
    ● The Christ University Library system with 5 specialized library centres in 3 campuses houses more than 250,000 books, 600 periodicals, 27 newspapers , 5000 CD’s and DVD’s and subscribes to 15 online databases with campus-wide access and has more than 250 computer terminals to facilitate access to the internet.
    ● Every student is assigned a Christ University Email ID with 7 GB space ; students can access their personal details and the internet using their register number and password. The website also acts as a portal of information and accessibility which are hosted from local servers in the campus.
    ● The Christ University Hospitality system consists of Jonas Hall (340 capacity girls hostel) and Kuriakose Hall (350 capacity boys hostel ) inside the Hosur Road Campus , Christ Hall (270 capacity boys hostel ) in Bannerghatta Road Campus , Devadan Hall (1000 capacity boys hostel) and for about 200 girl students at the Kengeri Campus (Mysore Road). The University provides more than 25 restaurants and eateries along with the Campus and the reprographic store to take care of the student’s food and academic needs.
    ● The University also hosts 5 Multi-gym fitness centers separately for men and women at both the campuses, ample games and sports facilities , counselling centre with 12 professionally trained counsellors , Doctor on Campus, 3 prayer halls for silent prayer and reflection, etc make up the Health and Wellbeing Support System.
    ● The Campus has all the facilities to nurture the holistic development of its students and the University is adjudged as one of the 10 best in the country by the ranking agencies.
